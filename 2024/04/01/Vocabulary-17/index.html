Vocabulary 17

本文最后更新于 2024年4月1日 下午

  1. availability/əˌveɪ.ləˈbɪl.ə.t̬i/

    可用性

    The ready availability of guns has contributed to the escalating violence.

    人们太容易弄到枪支，这也成了暴力事件激增的一个诱因。

    Abortion rates are high because the availability of contraceptives/ˌkɑːn.trəˈsep.tɪv/ is limited.

    由于避孕药具的获得途径有限，堕胎率居高不下。

  2. reliability/rɪˌlaɪ.əˈbɪl.ə.t̬i/

    Rolls-Royce cars are famous for their quality and reliability.

    劳斯莱斯汽车以其质量和可靠性而闻名。

    The entire office is dependent on her reliability and competence.

    整个办公室的人都依赖于她的可靠和能力。

    (un)reliable

    Is your watch reliable?

    你的表准吗？

    reliable information

    可靠信息

    Gideon is very reliable - if he says he’ll do something, he’ll do it.

    吉迪恩为人很可靠——他说到做到。

  3. toothpaste 牙膏

    toothbrush 牙刷

  4. invaluable

    extremely useful

    The new job will provide you with invaluable experience.

    这份新工作将使你获得宝贵的经验。

    Such data will prove invaluable to/for researchers.

    这些数据对于研究者来说将是非常宝贵的。

  5. surefire

    The film looks a surefire Oscar winner.

    看来这部电影一定会得奥斯卡奖。

    Running into the street like that is a surefire way to get hurt.

    那样跑到路上去肯定会受伤的。

  6. obsolete/ˌɑːb.səlˈiːt/

    过时的

    Gas lamps became obsolete when electric lighting was invented.

    电灯发明以后煤气灯就被淘汰了。

  7. jumping-off point

    起点

    Using his daughter’s case as a jumping-off point, he described a justice system that ignores the rights of the victim.

    他从他女儿的事情说起，描述了无视受害者权利的司法体系。

  8. wearable

    （服饰）舒适的，实用的，适宜穿戴的

    Unlike a lot of women’s fashion magazines, it features clothes that are both affordable and wearable.

    与很多女性时尚杂志不一样，它介绍的衣服既不太贵又适宜穿。

    （科技产品）可穿戴的

    Wearable technology such as “smart clothes” can be used to monitor heart rate.

    像“智能衣服”这样的可穿戴产品将可以用来监测心跳频率。

    可穿戴的科技产品

    The clothing center provides homeless people with good, clean coats, boots, and other wearables.

    服装中心为无家可归的人提供优质、干净的外套、靴子和其他衣物。

    Packed with the same computing power as some laptops, wearables are still too expensive for average consumers.

    可穿戴设备与一些笔记本电脑具有同等的运算能力，但它们对于普通消费者来说仍然过于昂贵。

  9. decisive(ly)

    You need to be more decisive.

    你要更果断一些。

    These results could prove decisive in establishing the criminal’s identity.

    这些结果对确定罪犯的身份将具有决定性的作用。

    a decisive victory

    决定性的胜利

    If we had acted earlier and more decisively it might not have come to this.

    如果我们更早更果断采取行动，事情可能不会走到这一步。

    He decisively changes lane with no signal.

    他果断地在没打信号的情况下转换车道。

    The electorate/iˈlek.tɚ.ət/ voted decisively to change the country’s electoral system.

    选民果断投票以改变该国的选举制度。

    My bet is that she will decisively win the next election.

    我敢打赌，她将决定性地赢得下届选举。

  10. prodigy/ˈprɑː.də.dʒi/ 神童

    The 16-year-old tennis prodigy is the youngest player ever to reach the Olympic finals.

    这位 16 岁的网球天才是迄今打进奥林匹克决赛的最年轻的选手。

    He read in the paper about a mathematical prodigy who was attending university at the age of twelve.

    他在报纸上看到一则 12 岁就上大学的数学奇才的报道。

  11. world-class 世界一流的

    a world-class athlete/performance

    世界一流的运动员／表演

  12. clueless/ˈkluː.ləs/

    Most people are completely clueless about tide directions and weather conditions.

    大多数人对潮汐的方向和天气状况一无所知。

  13. novice/ˈnɑː.vɪs/

    新手，初学者

    a person who is not experienced in a job or situation

    I’ve never driven a car before - I’m a complete novice.

    我以前从未开过车——完全是个新手。

    This plant can be difficult for novice gardeners to grow.

    让园艺新手来培育这种植物是相当有难度的。

  14. trivial/ˈtrɪv.i.əl/

    微不足道的，琐碎的；没有价值的

    I don’t know why he gets so upset about something so trivial.

    我不明白他因何为芝麻大的事儿也如此烦恼。

    Sexual harassment/həˈræs.mənt/ in the workplace is not a trivial matter.

    工作场所的性骚扰可不是件小事。

    Getting computers to understand human language is not a trivial problem.

    让计算机懂得人类语言可不是一个容易解决的问题。

  15. destiny/ˈdes.tɪ.ni/

    the things that will happen in the future

    The destiny of our nation depends on this vote!

    我们国家的前途取决于这次选举！

    She felt that her destiny had been shaped by her gender.

    她觉得自己的性别已经决定了自己的命运。

    People want to control/determine/take charge of their own destinies.

    人们想要掌控自己的命运。

    You can’t fight destiny.

    你无法与命运之神抗争。

  16. fate

    缘分；命中注定的事；命运

    We want to decide our own fate.

    我们想决定自己的命运。

    His fate is now in the hands of the jury.

    现在他的命运掌握在陪审团的手中。

    The disciples/dɪˈsaɪ.pəl/ were terrified that they would suffer/meet the same fate as Jesus.

    门徒们很害怕遭遇和耶稣一样的命运。

    When we met again by chance in Cairo, I felt it must be fate.

    当我们再度在开罗偶遇时，我觉得这一定是天意了。

    Fate has brought us together.

    天意让我们相聚。

  17. initial

    My initial surprise was soon replaced by delight.

    我最初的惊讶很快变成了兴奋。

    Initial reports say that seven people have died, though this has not yet been confirmed.

    最初的报道说有 7 人丧生，但这一消息还未得到证实。

    He wrote his initials, P.M.R., at the bottom of the page.

    他在页尾写上了他名字的首字母 P.M.R.。

    Paul M. Reynolds refused to say what the initial “M” stood for.

    保罗‧M‧雷诺兹拒绝说出首字母 M 代表什么意思。

    They carved their initials into a tree.

    他们把自己名字的首字母刻在了树上。

  18. visa/ˈviː.zə/ 签证

    We travelled to Argentina on a tourist visa.

    我们持旅游签证到阿根廷旅行。

    I’ve applied for an extension to my visa .

    My visa still hasn’t come through.

  19. passport 护照

    Many refugees/ˌref.jəˈdʒiː/ have arrived at the border without passports.

    许多难民到达边境时身上没有护照。

  20. cash

    Do you have any cash on you?

    你身上有现金吗？

    Will you pay by credit card or in cash?

    你是刷信用卡还是付现金？

    He says he wants cash in advance before he’ll do the job.

    他说他要先拿到预付现金才肯干活。

    Would you cash a cheque for me?

    你能帮我兑张支票吗？
